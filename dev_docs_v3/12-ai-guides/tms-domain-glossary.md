# TMS Domain Glossary

> AI Dev Guide | Full glossary of TMS/3PL domain terms

---

## A

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Accessorial** | Any charge beyond basic line haul transportation (detention, liftgate, fuel surcharge, etc.) |
| **Aging** | Categorizing accounts receivable by how long invoices have been outstanding (30/60/90/90+ days) |
| **Appointment** | Scheduled pickup or delivery time at a facility. Some facilities require appointments. |
| **AR (Accounts Receivable)** | Money owed to the broker by customers |
| **AP (Accounts Payable)** | Money owed by the broker to carriers |
| **Authority** | Legal permission from FMCSA to operate as a motor carrier or freight broker |

## B

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Bill of Lading (BOL)** | Legal document itemizing freight being shipped. Created at pickup, signed at delivery. |
| **Blind Shipment** | Shipper or consignee identity is hidden from the carrier |
| **Brokerage** | The business of arranging freight transportation without owning trucks |
| **Bumping** | Removing a carrier from an assigned load (before pickup) |

## C

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Carrier** | Company or individual that owns trucks and physically transports freight |
| **CDL** | Commercial Driver's License (Class A, B, or C) |
| **Check Call** | Status update from or about a driver during transit |
| **Claims** | Damage or loss claims filed against carriers |
| **Commodity** | The type of goods being shipped |
| **Consignee** | The party receiving the freight at destination |
| **Consignor** | The party sending the freight (shipper) |
| **CSA** | Compliance, Safety, Accountability -- FMCSA safety scoring program |
| **Cross-dock** | Facility where freight is transferred between trucks without storage |

## D

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Deadhead** | Miles driven without freight (empty miles). Carriers minimize this. |
| **Delivery** | The act of unloading freight at the consignee's facility |
| **Demurrage** | Charge for holding a container/trailer beyond the agreed time |
| **Detention** | Charge for keeping a truck waiting beyond free time at pickup or delivery ($75/hr standard) |
| **Dispatch** | Assigning a carrier/driver to a load and providing pickup/delivery instructions |
| **DOT Number** | US Department of Transportation registration number |
| **Drayage** | Short-distance transport, typically to/from ports, rail yards, or cross-docks |
| **Drop Trailer** | Leaving a trailer at a facility for loading/unloading, picking it up later |
| **Dry Van** | Standard enclosed 53-foot trailer. Most common equipment type (~70% of loads). |
| **Dwell Time** | Time a truck spends waiting at a facility |

## E-F

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **ELD** | Electronic Logging Device. Required for tracking driver Hours of Service. |
| **Equipment** | The type of trailer needed (dry van, reefer, flatbed, etc.) |
| **Expedited** | Time-sensitive shipment requiring faster-than-normal transit |
| **Factoring** | Carrier sells invoices to a third party for immediate payment at a discount |
| **Flatbed** | Open-top trailer for oversized or heavy freight |
| **FMCSA** | Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration. Regulates carriers and brokers. |
| **Free Time** | Hours allowed at a facility before detention charges begin (typically 2 hours) |
| **Freight Class** | NMFC classification (1-500) based on density, handling, liability, and stowability |
| **FSC (Fuel Surcharge)** | Variable charge tied to fuel prices, added to line haul |
| **FTL (Full Truckload)** | Shipment that fills an entire trailer |

## G-L

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **GPS Tracking** | Satellite-based location tracking of trucks in transit |
| **Hazmat** | Hazardous materials requiring special handling, placards, and driver endorsements |
| **HOS** | Hours of Service. Federal rules limiting driver operating time. |
| **Intermodal** | Using multiple transportation modes (truck + rail, truck + ship) |
| **Lane** | A specific origin-destination route (e.g., "Dallas TX to Chicago IL") |
| **Layover** | Overnight wait at a facility. Charge: typically $350/day. |
| **LTL (Less Than Truckload)** | Shipment that doesn't fill a full trailer. Combined with other freight. |
| **Line Haul** | Base transportation charge from origin to destination, excluding accessorials |
| **Load** | A single shipment of freight. The core operational unit in a TMS. |
| **Load Board** | Marketplace where available loads are posted for carriers to bid on |
| **Lumper** | Third-party worker who unloads freight. Charge is passed through to customer. |

## M-P

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Margin** | Revenue minus cost. Broker's profit on a load. |
| **MC Number** | Motor Carrier number issued by FMCSA |
| **NMFC** | National Motor Freight Classification |
| **Owner-Operator** | Independent driver who owns their own truck |
| **Pallet** | Standard shipping platform (48x40 inches). Freight is stacked on pallets. |
| **Pickup** | The act of loading freight at the shipper's facility |
| **POD (Proof of Delivery)** | Signed document confirming delivery. Required before invoicing. |
| **PRO Number** | Progressive number assigned by carrier for shipment tracking |

## Q-S

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Quick Pay** | Paying a carrier faster than standard terms in exchange for a discount fee |
| **Rate Con** | Rate Confirmation. Agreement between broker and carrier on rate and terms. |
| **Reefer** | Refrigerated trailer for temperature-sensitive freight |
| **SAFER** | Safety and Fitness Electronic Records system (FMCSA database) |
| **Settlement** | Payment to a carrier for a delivered load |
| **Shipper** | The company sending freight (the customer) |
| **Step Deck** | Trailer with a lower rear deck for taller freight |
| **Stop** | A pickup, delivery, or intermediate point on a load's route |

## T-Z

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Tarping** | Covering flatbed freight with a tarp. Extra charge: $75-150. |
| **Team Drivers** | Two drivers alternating to keep the truck moving 24/7 |
| **Tender** | Offering a load to a carrier for acceptance |
| **TMS** | Transportation Management System. Software for managing freight operations. |
| **TONU** | Truck Ordered Not Used. Fee when a load is cancelled after carrier dispatch ($250-500). |
| **Tracking** | Real-time monitoring of shipment location and ETA |
| **Transit Time** | Duration from pickup to delivery |
| **W-9** | IRS tax form required from carriers before payment |
